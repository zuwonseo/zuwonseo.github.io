San Francisco, US

Our first city is San Francisco, United States! Its coordinates are 37.5°N, 122.5°W. It is located iin Northern California on a peninsula between the Pacific Ocean and San Francisco Bay.

San Francisco has a mediterranean type climate with only two seasons, winter, and summer. The summers are hot, mild, and dry. In the winter there is more precipitation . During the year the temperature varies from 7°C to 22°C but overall does not change drastically through the seasons.

Vegetation Profile

San Francisco is hilly with many different types of vegetation. They have coastal scrub with different types of bushes and small plants, oak woodlands dominated by oak trees, saltwater wetlands, and freshwater wetlands. Lastly, most of the city is occupied by grasslands which have different types of annual grasses. San Francisco is also home to lots of wildlife including, coyotes, hawks, deer, banana slugs and many more.

Natural Resources

San Francisco produces natural gas and petroleum. Limestone, chalk and clay can also be found and they can be used to make cement but to get these raw materials, they must be crushed and grinded. These methods leave a big environmental footprint. San Francisco gets natural gas from natural gas basins.The natural gas is used for heating, water heating, and cooking, but the natural gas is transported through pipes which results in cutting down the environment to do it.

Santiago, Chile

Next city is Santiago, Chile. Its coordinates are 33.5°S, 70.7°W and it is located in Chile's Maipo Valley surrounded by the Andes Mountain Range, about 100 km east of the Pacific Ocean coast. The distance between Santiago and San Francisco is 5,935 km.

Santiago has a cool semi-arid climate. With mediterranean patterns with warm dry summers and cold humid winters. It doesn't have the four seasons. On the hottest days, temperatures reach up to 35°C.

Vegetation Profile

Santiago varies from shrubland to grasslands. Other important formations are the bamboo. Animals such as guanacos, vicunas, alpacas, armadillos, foxes, opossums, deers and pumas live here. Plants such as White star magnolia and pink magnolia with a weeping willow and below the flame tree in Los Dominicos.

Natural Resources

Santiago de Chile is the top copper producer in the world with 29 percent of global copper. It also produces large amounts of fresh fruits like avocados , blueberries, kiwifruit and pears. Chile's main agricultural exports include salmon, pork, poultry, and forestry products. A side effect of growing avocados is that in Chile there’s not enough water for the citizens and they use a great part of that water for the avocado plantations, to grow a kilogram of avocados 2000 litres of water are needed. So there’s a dilema about if they should keep using the water for the avocados or save it for the people.

Venice, Italy

Next up is Venice, Italy, located on 45.4° N, 12.3° E. It is in northern Italy, on the northwestern part of the Adriatic Sea. It is 12,084 km away from santiago.

Venice has a mid-latitude, four season humid subtropical climate. The summers are warm and humid, the winters are very cold, and it is partly cloudy year round. Over the course of the year, the temperature typically varies from 0°C to 23°C and is rarely below -4°C or above 32°C.

Vegetation Profile

Venice has four species of plant Phanerogams that are mostly found in the Venice lagoon. These plants are called Sea-wrack, Sea-grass, most of those are found in the water covered by over a metre. Additionally, Zostera noltii Hornem grass, and Sea-madder occur near the seabanks. Wild animals such as cormorants, egrets, grebes, herons, hawks, falcons, black-legged stilts, ducks, plovers, and even pink flamingos lives in Venice.

Natural Resources

In Venice we find a lot of natural resources, some of them are oil reserves, fish, coal, and marble. They also have a lot of metals and minerals. They have fishing valley agriculture systems in the Venetian lagoon. The oil reserves have negative impacts on the surrounding land and marine ecosystems. Mining coal can lead to deforestation, erosion, and many others.

Cairo, Egypt

The fourth city is Cairo, Egypt, located on 30.0° N, 31.2° E. It is in northeastern Egypt, primarily on the eastern shore of the Nile River. 2373 km is the distance between Cairo and Venice.

Cairo’s climate is dry, hot, and dominated by desert. During mild winters there is rain falling along the coastal areas, and summers are extremely hot. Prevailing winds change daytime temperatures.

Vegetation Profile

The natural vegetation of Cairo varies because of the lack of rainfall. Where some water is found, there are perennials and grasses. During spring, the coastal strip is full of plant life. The eastern desert has sparse rainfall, supporting vegetation like tamarisk, acacia, and markeh, as well as thorny shrubs, small succulents, and aromatic herbs. Horses, camels, buffalos and donkeys are examples of wild animals in Cairo.

Mining coal can lead to deforestation, erosion, and many others.

Seoul, South Korea

Fifth city is Seoul, South Korea. Its coordinates are 37.6° N, 126.9° E. It is in northwestern part of the country, close to the border with North Korea, and the Yellow Seashores. This city is 8,482 km away from the previous city.

Seoul’s climate is overall humid but with a very dry winter. It has four seasons, and temperature changes can be extreme, with really hot summers and extremely cold winters. It is a subtropical transitional climate.

Vegetation Profile

Seoul had hot and humid summers which do very well for a varied range of vegetation. 4,500 plant species are known there. They have deciduous forests mainly oak, maple trees, and evergreen broad-leaf trees. Seoul is also home to many wildlife including tigers, leopards, lynx, and bears.

Natural Resources

South Korea is where you can find coal, iron ore, graphite, gold, silver, tungsten, lead and zinc. This includes two-thirds of the total value of mineral resources. Seoul is also famous for pears, grapes, mandarin oranges, apples, peaches, corn and more. Seoul faces challenges like urbanizations, floods, pollution, resource overuse, snowstorms, landslides, drought and more.

Melbourne, Austrailia

Last city is Melbourne, Australia. Its coordinates are 37.8° S, 144.9° E. Its located in southeastern part of mainland Australia, within the state of Victoria. If you travel 8,575 km from Seoul, you can get here.

Melbourne has a mild climate with four distinct seasons. It has hot summers, warm springs, mild autumns, and cool winters, and overall has a variable climate. Throughout the year the temperature varies from 14°C to 25°C and the precipitation stays consistent throughout. Melbourne's climate is subtropical.

Vegetation Profile

Melbourne is mostly dominated by Shrubby Dry Forest, Damp Forest, Cool Temperate Rainforests, and Montane Dry forests. Plants such as gum trees, acacia, spider flowers, kangaroo paws, and native bluewell grow throughout Melbourne. They have lots of wildlife including Southern Koalas, Tasmanian Devils, wombats, kangaroos and a wide range of birds.

Natural Resources

In Melbourne, Australia, the ground is barren & not ideal for farming. There are a lot of minerals found here such as gold, silver, uranium, coal, copper, & iron ore. It leads the world in mining bauxite, titanium, and diamonds. In the non-desert areas a lot of grains are grown, like wheat, barley, rye, rice, chickpeas, and lentils. The bad thing about the mining boom in Australia is that 75% of mines have been closed prematurely or in an unplanned way, leaving them either abandoned or not properly rehabilitated.This is damaging the landscape and soils which is why it is a very important issue at the moment.
